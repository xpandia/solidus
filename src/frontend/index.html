The Problem

Institutions can't trust
today's stablecoin rails.

$4T+

in institutional capital sits on the sidelines.

Traditional stablecoin infrastructure was built for retail DeFi, not for institutions managing billions. There's no compliance at the protocol level, no programmable controls, and reserve attestation is a quarterly PDF.

Institutions need infrastructure that meets their standards from day one — not workarounds bolted on after the fact.

🚫

No Compliance Layer

KYC/AML enforcement doesn't exist at the protocol level. Regulatory exposure is unbounded.

⚠️

No Programmable Controls

No whitelisting, velocity limits, or role-based permissions. Treasurers get a raw token and hope for the best.

👁

No Real-Time Transparency

Reserve attestations come quarterly as PDFs. Institutions need real-time, on-chain proof of backing — every block.

How It Works

Three steps. That's it.

Solidus turns complex institutional compliance into a simple, programmable workflow on Solana.

Step 01

Configure

Define compliance rules — KYC tiers, transfer limits, whitelisted counterparties — through the dashboard or SDK.

Step 02

Issue

Stablecoins are minted through the Solidus program with compliance rules embedded via Token-2022 extensions.

Step 03

Operate

Every transfer is validated on-chain against your ruleset. Reserve status updated in real-time via oracles. Full audit trail.

Features

Everything institutions need.
Nothing they don't.

Built for treasurers, compliance teams, and protocol engineers who refuse to compromise.

Token-2022 Native

Built directly on Solana's Token Extensions — transfer hooks, confidential transfers, and metadata extensions, not wrappers.

On-Chain KYC

Credential verification embedded at the mint level. Only verified addresses can hold, send, or receive. Non-negotiable.

Live Reserve Proof

Oracle-powered reserve attestation updated every block. Not quarterly PDFs — real-time, on-chain, verifiable by anyone.

Velocity Controls

Set transfer limits per wallet, per time window. Rate limiting at the protocol level — not an afterthought.

Role-Based Access

Issuer, compliance officer, treasurer — each role has distinct permissions enforced by the program. Separation of duties, on-chain.

Full Audit Trail

Every mint, burn, transfer, and policy change is recorded immutably. Compliance teams get a complete, exportable history.
